Introduction

Shohei Ohtani is a Japanese professional baseball player, known for his exceptional skills as both a pitcher and hitter. Born on July 5, 1994, in Oshu, Japan, Ohtani has become one of baseball's most prominent two-way players, often compared to Babe Ruth.

Early Life

From a young age, Ohtani showed a passion for baseball. He excelled in both pitching and hitting during his high school years at Hanamaki Higashi High School. He quickly gained attention for his fastball, which exceeded 99 mph at the time.

Transition to the MLB

After a successful start to his career in Japan's Nippon Professional Baseball (NPB) league with the Hokkaido Nippon-Ham Fighters, Ohtani signed with the Los Angeles Angels in 2017 to pursue his dream of playing in Major League Baseball (MLB).
